maximum mutual information principle by Linsker(1988)

The synaptic connections of a multilayered neural network develop in such a way as to maximize the amount of information that is perserved when signals are transformed at each stage of the network, subject to certain constraints.

perceptual system by Attneave(1954)1

A major function of the perceptual machinery is to strip away some of the reduncdancy of stimulation, to describe or encode information in a form more economical than that in which it impinges on the receptors.

information, or the information gained after observing the event $X = p_k$ with probability $p_k$, $$ I(p_k) = log(\frac{1}{p_k}) = -log(p_k) $$

entropy

the average amount of information conveyed per message. $$ H(X) = \mathbb{E}[I(x_k)] $$

maximum-entropy principle by Jaynes(1957)

When an information is made on the basis of incomplete information, it should by drawn the probability distribution that maximizes the entropy, subject to the constraint on the distribution.

by Shore and Johson(1980)

Given the prior knowledge in the form of constraints, there is only one distribution satisfying these constraints that can be chosen by a procedure that satisfies the "consistency axioms"; this unique distribution is defined by maximizing entropy.

mutual information $$ I(\mathbf{X};\mathbf{Y}) = h(\mathbf{X}) - h(\mathbf{X}|\mathbf{Y})\\ = h(\mathbf{Y}) - h(\mathbf{Y}|\mathbf{X})\\ = h(\mathbf{X}) + h(\mathbf{X}) - h(\mathbf{X,Y}) $$
